As a public school teacher, I never expected my own children to attend anything other than traditional public school. Our daughter changed that narrative.

Our daughter was born with Spina Bifida and has undergone 11 spinal surgeries. Because of this, she has always struggled to physically keep up with her peers, which created challenges for her at school. As time went on, additional medical concerns made learning even more difficult, and eventually she was diagnosed with ADHD. That diagnosis was a wake-up call for us and explained many of the academic struggles she had experienced over the years.

She is kind, quiet, and incredibly sweet. Because of that, she often did not receive the extra attention she needed in a busy classroom setting. She was sometimes labeled as lazy or disorganized when, in reality, she was struggling and needed support tailored to the way she learns.

It was never that her school failed her. In many ways, the system simply was not designed to meet every child’s individual needs. Not all students learn the same way, and she needed a learning environment that allowed for more time, flexibility, and individualized support.

She loved the social side of school, and because I worked full time, we continued with traditional public school throughout elementary school. However, when it came time for middle school, we made the decision to enroll her in virtual school full time.

She is still a public school student, but now she has the ability to slow down when she gets stuck and truly work toward understanding concepts before moving on. Because I also work for a virtual school, we had the advantage of understanding how the system works and how to better support her learning at home.

For example, when she struggles in math, we often use IXL for additional practice before she takes a quiz on a topic. The ESE support she receives has also been incredible. She now gets individualized attention that meets her where she is and helps build both her skills and confidence.

Most importantly, she works incredibly hard. While learning may not always come easily to her, she thrives in grit and perseverance. She sticks with difficult concepts and is willing to put in the extra effort needed to master them.

Traditional school was not the right fit for her learning needs, and that is okay. The demands placed on teachers today are enormous, and it is not always possible for every child to receive truly individualized instruction in a classroom setting.

We are fortunate to live in a place where families have educational options, and we feel incredibly grateful to have the ability to support her learning from home while still remaining connected to public education.

Raising children is hard, and finding the right educational path can be difficult. For our family, this has been a journey of learning, flexibility, and discovering that sometimes children thrive when given a different environment to grow in.

We thought our educational journey had settled into place. Looking back now, we had no idea that our son would soon lead us into an entirely new chapter of learning and growth.
